Nabeyaki Udon

(Taken from: Just One Cookbook )

Description

Nabeyaki Udon (鍋焼きうどん) is a hot udon noodle soup traditionally served in individual donabe (earthenware pot) or iron pots.

Besides udon noodles, the soup usually includes chicken, kamaboko (fish cake), mushrooms, and vegetables such as spinach, long green onion (negi), and carrot.

In addition, an egg and a large shrimp tempura is served on top.

The literal translation for nabeyaki udon is, you've guessed it, “cook in hotpot udon”. The recipe is easy and doesn't take that much effort to make. Simply make dashi (soup stock), add udon and any other ingredients you like in a pot, and cook!

Ingredients

  • 4 dried shiitake mushrooms
  • ½ cup water (for soaking dried shiitake mushrooms)
  • 1 stalk spinach (rinsed)
  • 2.5 cm carrot
  • 4 slices kamaboko (fish cake)
  • ⅓ shimeji mushrooms (1 oz, 28 g; bottom trimmed)
  • 15 cm negi (long green onion) (use white part, or leeks + green onions/scallions)
  • 1 chicken thigh (4-5oz, 113-142g)
  • 2 servings udon noodles (6.3 oz/180 g dry udon noodles; 1.1 lb/500 g frozen or parboiled udon noodles)
  • 2 large eggs (50 g each w/o shell)
  • 2 shrimp tempura (see below for instructions; you can buy frozen shrimp tempura and follow instructions to reheat as well)

For Udon Soup

  • 720 ml dashi (Japanese soup stock) (I used the standard kombu + katsuobushi awase dashi; use kombu dashi for vegan/vegetarian)
  • 80 ml shiitake dashi (reserved from rehydrating dried shiitake, see instructions)
  • 2 Tbsp mirin
  • 1 ½ Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp kosher salt (Diamond Crystal; use half for table salt)

For Shrimp Tempura

  • 2 shrimp
  • 25 g tempura batter mix
  • 40 ml cold water or sparkling water
  • potato starch or cornstarch (for dusting)

Steps

To Prepare the Ingredients

  1. Gather all the ingredients.

  2. Soak dried shiitake mushrooms in water for 15 minutes to rehydrate.

    You need just enough water to cover the mushrooms and place a smaller bowl on top to keep the mushrooms submerged in water.

  3. Squeeze the excess water out of rehydrated shiitake mushrooms.

    Cut off the stems if there are any and score a cross on top like this and set aside (use it later for topping).

    Reserve the leftover soaking liquid (shiitake dashi) for the udon soup by straining it through a mesh strainer to remove any grit or impurities before using it.

  4. In lightly salted boiling water, blanch the spinach starting from the stem side for 1 minute.

  5. Soak the spinach in iced water to prevent overcooking.

    Squeeze water out and cut into 1 ½” (4 cm) pieces.

  6. Slice the carrot.
  7. Cut kamaboko into thin slices and break shimeji mushrooms into small pieces.
  8. Slice the long green onion diagonally and cut the chicken into 2.5cm pieces.

To Make the Udon Soup

  1. Combine the dashi and shiitake dashi in a small pot.

    Bring it to a boil over medium-high heat.

  2. Once boiling, add mirin, soy sauce, and salt.

    When boiling again, turn off the heat and set aside.

To Make Shrimp Tempura

  1. Follow this instruction to clean the shrimp and make them straight.

  2. Combine the tempura batter mix and water.

    Lightly dust the shrimp with potato starch/cornstarch before dredging the shrimp in the batter.

    Deep fry at 170-180 ºC (340-350 ºF) until golden brown.

    If you want to make the tempura batter from scratch and learn tips on how to deep fry the shrimp, you can read the recipe here.

To Prepare the Udon Noodles

  1. Bring a large pot of water to boil.

    Cook the frozen udon noodles in boiling water for 30 seconds (no need to defrost). My favourite udon is the frozen Sanuki Udon.

    If you use dry noodles, follow the package instructions.

    Drain the water and chill in ice water to prevent the noodles from cooking further.

To Assemble

  1. Put all ingredients into an earthen donabe clay pots (or use any big pot).

  2. Add udon soup and cover with the lid.

    Bring the soup to a boil over medium-high heat.

    When it comes to a boil, leave the lid slightly open to let some steam out, or it will overflow.

    Lower the heat and simmer to cook until the chicken is cooked through.

  3. Add the eggs and spinach

    Cover to cook until the egg is done to your liking.

  4. Add shrimp tempura on top and serve.

    Sprinkle shichimi togarashi (Japanese seven spice) on top if you like the soup spicy.
